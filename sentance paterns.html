JLPT N5 Japanese Sentence Patterns

Pattern: ～は～です

This pattern is used to say "A is B", where A and B are nouns.

Example:

私は学生です。 (わたしはがくせいです) – "I am a student."

Note: "は" is the topic marker and "です" is the copula, often translated as "is" or "am".

Pattern: ～に行く

This pattern is used to say "to go to [place]."

Example:

学校に行きます。 (がっこうにいきます) – "I am going to school."

Note: "に" indicates the direction of the action, and "行く" means "to go".

Pattern: ～が好き

This pattern expresses "to like [something]."

Example:

私は日本語が好きです。 (わたしはにほんごがすきです) – "I like Japanese."

Note: "が" is the subject marker and "好き" means "like".

Pattern: ～と～

This pattern is used to say "A and B". It connects two nouns or actions.

Example:

私は日本語と英語を話します。 (わたしはにほんごとえいごをはなします) – "I speak Japanese and English."

Pattern: ～がある

This pattern is used to express existence for inanimate objects. It means "There is [something]."

Example:

机の上に本があります。 (つくえのうえにほんがあります) – "There is a book on the desk."

Pattern: ～がいる

This pattern is used to express existence for living things (people, animals). It means "There is [someone/thing]."

Example:

公園に犬がいます。 (こうえんにいぬがいます) – "There is a dog in the park."

Pattern: ～へ行く

This pattern is used to express "going to [place]" where "へ" indicates the direction.

Example:

私は図書館へ行きます。 (わたしはとしょかんへいきます) – "I am going to the library."

Pattern: ～の

This pattern is used to show possession or to describe something related to something else. It translates to "of" or "’s".

Example:

これは私の本です。 (これはわたしのほんです) – "This is my book."

Pattern: ～を食べる

This pattern is used for "eating" something. "を" marks the direct object.

Example:

私はりんごを食べます。 (わたしはりんごをたべます) – "I eat an apple."

Pattern: ～を飲む

This pattern is used to express "drinking" something. "を" marks the object.

Example:

私はお茶を飲みます。 (わたしはおちゃをのみます) – "I drink tea."

Pattern: ～と話す

This pattern is used to express "talking to" someone.

Example:

私は友達と話します。 (わたしはともだちとはなします) – "I talk to my friend."

Pattern: ～を聞く

This pattern is used for asking or listening to something.

Example:

先生に質問を聞きます。 (せんせいにしつもんをききます) – "I ask the teacher a question."

Pattern: ～と一緒に

This pattern means "together with" someone.

Example:

私は母と一緒に買い物に行きます。 (わたしはははといっしょにかいものにいきます) – "I go shopping with my mother."

Pattern: ～から～まで

This pattern is used to express "from [A] to [B]".

Example:

東京から大阪まで電車で行きます。 (とうきょうからおおさかまででんしゃでいきます) – "I will go by train from Tokyo to Osaka."

Pattern: ～も

This pattern is used for "also" or "too".

Example:

私も日本語が好きです。 (わたしもにほんごがすきです) – "I also like Japanese."

Pattern: ～とき

This pattern is used for "when" something happens.

Example:

私は子供のとき、日本に行きました。 (わたしはこどものとき、にほんにいきました) – "When I was a child, I went to Japan."

Pattern: ～とき、～た

This pattern is used to express "when [A] happens, [B] happened".

Example:

試験のとき、緊張しました。 (しけんのとき、きんちょうしました) – "When I took the exam, I was nervous."

Pattern: ～まえに

This pattern is used for "before" an event or action.

Example:

寝るまえに、本を読みます。 (ねるまえに、ほんをよみます) – "I read a book before going to bed."

Pattern: ～あいだに

This pattern means "while" or "during" an action.

Example:

お風呂のあいだに、音楽を聴きます。 (おふろのあいだに、おんがくをききます) – "I listen to music while taking a bath."

Pattern: ～ようにする

This pattern is used to express efforts to do something.

Example:

毎日運動をするようにします。 (まいにちうんどうをするようにします) – "I try to exercise every day."
